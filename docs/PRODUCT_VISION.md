## Product Vision – FirstNightHotels

FirstNightHotels is a hotel search engine built around a simple thesis: **a freshly renovated 3-star hotel is often a better experience than a 20-year-old 5-star hotel**. Instead of optimizing purely for price, star rating, or review score, FirstNightHotels puts **freshness and renovation history** at the center of the discovery experience.

### Problem

Traditional hotel platforms optimize for:
- Star ratings that age slowly and are hard to interpret.
- Aggregated review scores that often mix old and outdated experiences with recent ones.
- Generic filters (price, distance, breakfast included) that don’t capture how “new” or “fresh” a hotel feels today.

Travelers who care about modern rooms, new bathrooms, and recently renovated properties have no simple way to:
- Quickly find newly opened or recently renovated hotels.
- See a clear renovation timeline.
- Compare the “age of experience” across hotels.

### Vision

FirstNightHotels becomes the **default place for travelers who care about “how new it feels”**. We:
- Index hotels with **opening dates** and **renovation dates** as first-class data.
- Make it trivial to **sort and filter by freshness**, for example:
  - Opened in the last 3 years.
  - Last major renovation within the last 5 years.
- Visualize a hotel’s **renovation history as a simple timeline**, so users can see how up to date rooms, lobby, spa, etc. are.

### Core Value Proposition

- **For travelers**: Find modern-feeling hotels faster and avoid “tired” properties even when ratings are high.
- **For hotels**: Surface renovation investments, not just price cuts and discounts.

### MVP Scope (high level)

For the initial MVP we focus on:
- Basic hotel search (location + dates).
- Sorting options:
  - By opening date.
  - By last renovation date.
- Simple freshness filters:
  - “Opened or renovated in the last X years” (for example 1, 3, 5).
- A basic hotel detail view showing:
  - Opening year.
  - Last major renovation year.
  - A short textual explanation of renovation history (if available).

The MVP optimizes for **speed and clarity** rather than breadth of features. Once users can reliably find “fresh” hotels in one or two markets, we iterate.

### Differentiation

FirstNightHotels does not try to compete on:
- The widest inventory on day one.
- The most complex loyalty or pricing logic.

Instead, we win when:
- Travelers feel like “this is the only place where I can reliably find fresh, renovated hotels”.
- Hotels that have invested in renovations see that investment reflected in visibility and conversions.
